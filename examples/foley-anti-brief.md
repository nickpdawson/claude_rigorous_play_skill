**Topic:** foley artistry
**Exercise:** Anti-Brief — design the worst possible version, extract what the failure reveals
**Constraint:** only questions (no statement may close on a period)
**Reflection:** Honest bridge

---

*Brief for the Worst Possible Foley Studio*

What if the floor were perfectly level?

What if every surface were the same surface — say, a 4×8 sheet of medium-density fibreboard, sealed, on rubber feet?

What if the props were photographs of props?

What if the foley artist had never broken a celery stalk, walked in snow, opened a wax-paper bag, or held a coconut?

What if the room had been tuned to remove all "unwanted" resonance — meaning, what if the room had been tuned to remove the room?

What if the brief said *"make it sound real"* and the budget said *eight minutes*?

What if the only direction the director ever gave was *"a little more"*?

What if every footstep had to match the picture exactly, but the picture was the wrong picture?

What if no one in the room had ever been outside?

---

**Honest bridge:**

The thing the anti-brief makes obvious: foley dies the moment you remove *texture you didn't plan for*. The level floor, the sealed MDF, the photograph of a prop — each one strips away a specific accident, and the sum of those accidents is what the ear actually hears as *real*. The room you tune to perfection is the room that sounds like nothing.

If your current work has a surface that's been sanded down too smooth — a UI, a doc, a process — the question worth asking isn't *what's missing?* It's *what accidents did I remove on purpose, and which ones should I put back?*
