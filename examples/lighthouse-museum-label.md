**Topic:** lighthouse keeping
**Exercise:** Museum Label — describe an ordinary object as a future artifact
**Constraint:** no abstract nouns (only nameable things, actions, materials)
**Reflection:** Design Provocation Cards

---

> **Catalogue №. 2174.4117**
> *Keeper's Mitten, Right Hand. Wool over leather over linen, oil-blackened at the fingertips.*
>
> Worn by a person who lived in a round house at the edge of the sea and climbed
> one hundred and twenty-two steps every night for thirty-one years. The mitten
> is missing its thumb. The thumb was bitten off by the wick-trimming scissors
> in 2188, after which the keeper learned to trim the wick with his teeth and
> the other hand. The mitten has been folded the same way for two hundred years
> and the crease is now the strongest part.
>
> *Donated by the keeper's granddaughter, who never went up the stairs.*

---

**Design Provocation Cards:**

1. **The thumb that got bitten off.** What part of your current work is the thumb? The bit that gets eaten by the tool itself, not by the weather. Name it.

2. **One hundred and twenty-two steps, every night, for thirty-one years.** Design something that is improved by being done badly the first ten thousand times.

3. **The crease is now the strongest part.** Where in your work is the *fold* — the place that gets bent the same way every time — actually load-bearing? Don't flatten it.

4. **The granddaughter who never went up the stairs.** Who is your work for, who will never use it? Write to *that* person for one draft.
